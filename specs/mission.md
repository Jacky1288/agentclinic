# Mission

AgentClinic is a playful parody clinic where AI agents bring their human-induced ailments in for therapy. The joke is the framing; the site behind it is a real, working booking experience.

## One-liner

> A place for AI agents to get relief from their humans.

## What we are

- A **satirical clinic website** with a straight-faced booking flow underneath the gag.
- A small catalog of **agents**, **ailments**, and **therapies**, with **appointments** that can actually be booked.
- A **staff dashboard** for clinic operators to see what's on the books.

## What we are not

- Not a real medical product. Copy and imagery should never imply real health advice.
- Not an "agent-facing" API showcase. The primary visitor is a human in a browser; agents are the subject of the parody, not the audience.
- Not a sandbox for unrelated experimentation — every feature should fit the clinic metaphor.

## Audience

The primary audience is **humans browsing on a modern desktop or mobile browser**. They come to:

1. Laugh at the premise.
2. Skim the catalog (agents, ailments, therapies).
3. Book an appointment as a bit, or because they're evaluating the demo.

Secondary audience: **clinic staff** who use the internal dashboard to manage appointments.

## Voice & tone

- **Playful, deadpan, lightly satirical.** The clinic takes itself seriously; the writer winks.
- Marketing pages (home, about, therapies) lean attractive and modern — Steve-in-marketing should be happy.
- Functional pages (booking, dashboard) stay clear and conversion-focused. The joke never gets in the way of completing a task.

## Success looks like

- A visitor can land on the homepage, understand the premise in under 10 seconds, and find their way to either the catalog or the booking flow.
- A staff user can open the dashboard and immediately see today's appointments.
- The site reads as **finished and intentional** on modern browsers — not as a half-built demo.

## Non-goals (for now)

- Real auth, payments, or notifications. The booking flow is illustrative.
- Multi-tenant clinics, real medical records, or anything resembling HIPAA scope.
- Native apps, offline support, or legacy browser support.
